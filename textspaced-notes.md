- "To discover a new system you can visit the same sublight section in the
  helm window and choose to explore in either direction. When you do this, your
  ship will travel until an undiscovered system is found which your ship will
  stop at."
- "But, you can also sometimes see undiscovered systems thanks to your
  sensors. Systems can be seen from different ranges depending on the brightness
  or size of its star. You can check for nearby undiscovered systems by opening
  the helm window, selecting nearby and then scrolling to the undiscovered
  section."
- "Discovering systems is quite important, as it provides more sites for
  building, charting, gathering, hunting and mining."
- "Your ship has a maximum fuel capacity, this is measured in light years, or
  LYs. When you travel at sublight speed which takes time, you will consume fuel
  at the exact rate of the distance you travel."
- "If you use instant FTL travel you will consume light drive charge, this
  charge is refilling automatically by converting fuel. How fast a ship can
  convert 0.1 light years of fuel to charge changes from ship to ship and is
  stated as a charge rate. This charge rate is measured in minutes... so a
  charge rate of 5 minutes means every 5 minutes your FTL charge increase by 0.1
  light years."
- "As you might expect you cannot use FTL travel if you don't have enough
  charge. If you run out of fuel, no charge will be generated. You can still
  travel at sublight, but your speed will be dire, capped at 0.1 light years per
  minute."
- "You'll notice that when you arrive at a system you'll always be in orbit of
  the systems star. A system is a collection of bodies like planets, wormholes,
  just things that are orbiting the star. Bodies will have commodities you can
  mine and structures can be built in orbit of bodies, on the surface of the
  body - or both really. If a system is unclaimed building a structure anywhere
  in the system regardless of what is built, will claim the system after 24
  hours."
- "You can view the details of a system by opening the helm window and
  selecting system. You'll be able to see the bodies, ships present and any
  rules set. Rules can be set by whichever faction owns the system, but I
  wouldn't worry about that for now, in this simulation AG Systems owns all the
  systems and the region. A region is just 50 LYs of space, the region owner is
  the faction that owns the most systems in a region."
- "Charting is important as once a planet is charted you'll be able to see the

  monetised by building a Toll Bridge structure to charge everyone that travels
  through. Just like FTL travel they provide almost instant travel to their
